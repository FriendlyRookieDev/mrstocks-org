eToro Review: Regulations, Reputation, Pros, Cons

When looking for a trader that permits cryptocurrencies trading, you’re likely to come across eToro sooner than later. The reason being, eToro is a top online brokerage for Forex, CFDs, ETFs, stocks, and cryptocurrencies in the world. But is it legit?

Today, we present you with a comprehensive review of this brokerage company, including its background, goals, customer support, and more. We aim to help you perceive a clear picture of the trading platform and, as such, make an informed decision.

Let’s dive in.

eToro Review: Background

eToro was first launched in Israel in 2007 under the name RetailFx. The platform was the work of three entrepreneurs who sought to make trading more accessible for everyone while reducing traders’ dependency on traditional financial institutions.

eToro began as an online platform with an intuitive interface. The interface featured graphs to represent financial instruments.

The eToro Trading Timeline

In 2009, the broker’s platform launched the WebTrader – the eToro platform with a wide range of professional tools for the everyday user.

In 2010/2011, eToro went through a significant update, including the launch of its famous copy trading platform.

In 2012, the company added stocks to its assets, such as commodities and currencies.

Initially, the platform was intended to be a forex trader, but the company introduced the option to trade stocks CFDs (explained in a later section) in 2013. Simultaneously, eToro launched the mobile app for Apple and Android systems. In addition, they dipped into cryptocurrencies, enabling users to trade Bitcoin through CFDs (Contract For Difference).

In 2015, eToro updated the interface to provide a more intuitive experience.

In 2016, they added CopyPortfolio – managed portfolios that buddle top traders or assets within a marketing strategy.

In 2017, they added support for cryptocurrency trading, which began with popular cryptocurrencies, such as XRP, Litecoin, and Ethereum.

In 2019, the brokerage launched eToroX, a full crypto to crypto exchange.

What eToro Offers Today

Today, eToro is known for stocks, commodities, Forex, CFDs with this provider, and cryptocurrency trading. The platform is easy to use. Its provision for copy trading makes it ideal for users interested in being copied for risk-controlled reasons.

Thus far, eToro USA, LLC has facilitated millions of trades thanks to its global reach. The company has registered subsidiaries in the UK, Australia, Cyprus, and the United States. It’s also focusing on entering the Asian markets soon.

But while most eToro’s trading services are allowed in many countries, there’s a significant number of countries that are banned from using the platform. They include Albania, Cuba, Canada, Iran, Iraq, Japan, Jamaica, Nicaragua, Syria, Serbia, and Pakistan.

So the background of eToro USA, LLC is quite enticing, but is the service legitimate?

eToro Review: Regulations, Reviews, and Reputation

To operate legally, an online broker requires permissions and licenses for each jurisdiction it avails its services.

In its case, eToro has secured a license from the FCA (Financial Conduct Authority) for its United Kingdon based subsidiaries. It has also acquired approval from the CySEC (Cyprus Securities and Exchange Commission) to covers its Cyprus-based users. Added to that is a license from the ASIC (Australian Securities and Investment Commission) covering Australian-based traders.

In addition, eToro has an FSCA license to provide regulations for its South Africa-based users, as well as other licenses, such as FinCEN from several American states.

In short, eToro and eToro wallet is well regulated by top financial authorities, making it safe and trustable with funds, personal information, social trading, and stock trading.

Besides regulation, a good reason to use eToro is that it’s well endorsed by most users across the globe – and understandably so. For one, that people trading eToro can feel secure since it takes precautions to safeguard its clients’ account, funds, and personal data. Traditional funds remain safe and secure in banks of the top tier. While some instruments have a high risk of losing, your money is safe through the eToro mobile app.

The copy trading feature is handy as trades can be copied at any scale. For instance, if you want to invest $10 000 but the trader you want to copy is investing $100,000, you can copy the trader at one-tenth of their original level.

eToro Review: Fees and Commissions

eToro features trading and non-trading fees.

Trading fees are costs incurred through trading. They include conversion fees, spreads, financing rates, and commissions.

Non-trading fees are costs incurred performing tasks with no direct relation to trading. They include withdrawal fees and inactivity fees.

Here’s a further breakdown of eToro fees for the most frequently traded financial instruments.

ETF fees and stock fees

eToro provides zero-commission stock and ETF trading for its EU users. And being a CDF broker, the platform enables users to trade with real stocks and ETFs when the leverage is set to one. For example:

5:1 for stock CFDs

20:1 for stock index CFDs

30:1 for Forex

Unfortunately, zero-commission trading is unavailable for users outside of the European Union.

ETF and stock fees are low – and the same goes for their spread. Spread equals the difference between the bid price and ask price. When the leverage position is set above one, the trading is with CFDs – which have a high risk of losing money. These accounts lose money due to the nature of the investment and poor research so make sure you understand how CFDs work before diving in.

Non-trading fees

Non-trading fees are often high as the user is charged $10 per month if they are inactive for 12 months. There is also a withdrawal fee of $5.

Cryptocurrency trading fee

It costs 5% (4% Simplex fee + 1% standard fee) to exchange fiat currency to cryptocurrency. While it costs 0.1% to convert cryptocurrency to cryptocurrency. This exchange requires a minimum of $20 and a maximum of $10,000 per transaction.

Incentives

eToro offers a popular four-level investor program ranging from cadet to elite. Here, the trader may be eligible to receive trading perks, including monthly payments, spread rebates, and management fees. This approach differs from an active trader program that requires a volume threshold or deposit.

VIP account

eToro provides a VIP club membership with five tiers ranging from gold to diamond. The diamond tier is for users with a constant balance of $5k to $250k.

The VIP account offers varying benefits depending on the tier. Such benefits include access to private signals and exclusive assets as well as a dedicated account manager.

eToro Review: Deposit and Withdrawal

eToro requires a minimum amount of initial deposit of $200 for an online banking transfer and $500 for a wire transfer. As a trader, you can only deposit up to $10,000 into your eToro account per day.

You can make deposits into the platform using the following options:

  • Skrill
  • Paypal
  • Credit or Debit card
  • Visa or Mastercard
  • Neteller
  • Webmoney
  • Giropay
  • Yandex
  • China Union Pay

The exchanges use Plaid, a third-party provider, to enable the client to open a new account with a direct connection to eToro. Note, a wire transfer requires some fees from the trader’s bank to be paid.

You can use the same option you used to deposit money into the eToro account to withdraw funds. The platform charges a $5 withdrawal fee. You can withdraw a minimum of $50. For more details on deposits and withdrawal fees, visit eToro’s fee page.

It is also worth noting that your account balance can decrease due to inactivity fees. For instance, if there have been no-trading activities for a year, you will be charged $10 each month. Fortunately, the eToro Club program provides traders with additional services and some discounts based on their account’s size.

Customer Support

Traders can connect with eToro via an online support forum. There, they can submit a ticket for various issues. eToro had availed a customer support line – however, most users claim that the calling option is no longer available.

There’s a separate customer service program through eToro partners.

As noted earlier, the customer reviews for eToro’s customer support are somewhat negative – primarily due to delayed responses.

Overall, submitting a complaint ticket is faster than reaching customer service directly. And you can rest assured that once your ticket is reached, they will do all they can to resolve your issues.

Account Opening

Just like any trading platform, eToro requires you to take several steps before you can begin trading. First, you will have to provide specific information. The account will then require verification with proof of government-issued identification, address, and a verified phone number.

Account verification can take up to two days. New users must prove their identity and residence by providing the following documents.

POI (proof of identity) document

Acceptable POI documents include passports, identification cards, and government-issued driver’s licenses.

POA (proof of address) document

The acceptable POA documents include:

  •  Bank statement
  • Utility bills such as water, gas, or electricity
  • Utility bill including gas, water, or electric

POIS (proof of identity and selfie) document

The photo must show you holding the POI document uploaded to the exchange.

Once the verification is done, you can now use the account to trade.

Users reports online banking as the easiest way to make deposits. Plaid, the third-party provider, enables clients to open a new account with a direct connection to eToro.
